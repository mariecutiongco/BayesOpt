Attempt at Bayesian Optimization for Nanotpography exploration

This notebook shows results for optimizing the morphome with respect to osteogenesis, but performed per geometry!

Notes specifically for Bayesian optimization protocol/algorithm: - Morphome data used: 243 features that were already used to model morphology to gene expression - Morphome is mean centered and scaled (mean = 0, sd=1) across both replicates - Note that Y data we are trying to optimize is just the weighted sum of bone gene expression, normalized to (0,1] by dividing by the maximum across 2 replicates - No separation made between SQ or HEX data but FLAT data are all excluded - Uses averaage of PCR data per replicate - The GP model used in the Bayesian optimization does not have the optimizer - The random search was performed by just getting a random datapoint within the available dataset, but the maximum Y is computed from a GP model using the random dataset
